Michael Jackson

The king of Pop

A summary of his life

  • Michael Joseph Jackson was born in Gary, Indiana, near Chicago, on August 29, 1958.
  • In 1964, Michael and Marlon joined the Jackson Brothers—a band formed by their father which included Jackie, Tito, and Jermaine—as backup musicians playing congas and tambourine.
  • In 1977, Jackson moved to New York City to star as the Scarecrow in The Wiz, a musical directed by Sidney Lumet.
  • Jackson's sixth album, Thriller, was released in late 1982. It was the best-selling album worldwide in 1983, and became the best-selling album of all time in the US and the best-selling album of all time worldwide, selling an estimated 70 million copies.
  • The success transformed Jackson into a dominant force in global pop culture, and cemented his status as the "king of pop".
